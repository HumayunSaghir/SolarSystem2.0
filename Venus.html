Venus — The Hottest Planet

Description

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and is often called Earth's "sister planet" because of their similar size and composition. However, despite these similarities, Venus has a very different environment. It is the hottest planet in our solar system, with surface temperatures reaching up to 465°C, hotter than Mercury, even though it is farther from the Sun. This is because Venus has a thick atmosphere made mostly of carbon dioxide, which traps heat through a strong greenhouse effect.
The surface of Venus is rocky and marked by vast plains, highland regions, and thousands of volcanoes. Many of these volcanoes are still believed to be active. Thick clouds of sulfuric acid cover the planet, making it impossible to observe its surface using regular telescopes. Instead, scientists have used radar mapping from spacecraft, such as NASA's Magellan mission, to study its terrain. The planet also rotates very slowly and in the opposite direction of most planets, including Earth.

Interesting Facts About Venus

  • Venus’s atmosphere is mostly carbon dioxide with clouds of sulfuric acid.
  • Surface temperatures reach up to 465°C (870°F).
  • A day on Venus (one rotation) is longer than its year (243 vs. 225 Earth days).
  • Venus is often called Earth’s “sister planet” due to its similar size.
  • It has no moons.
