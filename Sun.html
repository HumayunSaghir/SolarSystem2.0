Description:

The Sun is a giant glowing ball of hot gases located at the center of our Solar System. It contains about 99.86% of the total mass of the entire system and is the source of light and energy for all the planets, including Earth. It is classified as a G-type main-sequence star (also called a yellow dwarf) and lies approximately 150 million kilometers away from Earth.

Made mostly of hydrogen (around 74%) and helium (around 24%), the Sun generates its energy through nuclear fusion, a process that converts hydrogen into helium deep in its core. This process releases tremendous energy, which radiates outward through various layers of the Sun before reaching Earth as sunlight.

Structure of the Sun:

  1. Core – The central region where nuclear fusion takes place.
  2. Radiative Zone – A layer where energy slowly radiates outward.
  3. Convective Zone – A region where hot gases rise and cool gases sink.
  4. Photosphere – The visible surface of the Sun that emits sunlight.
  5. Chromosphere – A reddish layer above the photosphere.
  6. Corona – The outermost layer, visible during a total solar eclipse.

Interesting Facts About the Sun:

  • The Sun is about 4.6 billion years old, roughly the same age as the Solar System.
  • Light from the Sun takes around 8 minutes and 20 seconds to reach Earth.
  • Its core temperature can reach up to 15 million degrees Celsius, while the surface temperature is around 5,500°C.
  • The Sun rotates faster at the equator (about 25 days) than at the poles (about 35 days).
  • Eventually, the Sun will expand into a red giant and later become a white dwarf as it ends its life cycle.

The Sun Compared to Earth:

The Sun is vastly larger than Earth in every way. While the Earth has a diameter of about 12,742 kilometers, the Sun’s diameter is approximately 1.39 million kilometers, making it over 100 times wider than Earth. In terms of mass, the Sun weighs around 1.989 × 10³⁰ kilograms, whereas Earth’s mass is about 5.972 × 10²⁴ kilograms — meaning the Sun is over 300,000 times more massive.
The surface temperature of the Sun is around 5,500°C, while Earth’s average surface temperature is only about 15°C. At its core, the Sun reaches an astonishing 15 million degrees Celsius, a temperature far beyond anything on Earth. Both the Sun and Earth are around 4.5 to 4.6 billion years old, but the Sun is expected to live much longer than Earth. The average distance from Earth to the Sun is about 150 million kilometers, also known as 1 Astronomical Unit (AU)

Role in the Solar System:

  • Holds all planets, asteroids, and comets in orbit due to its powerful gravity.
  • Provides the light and heat necessary for life on Earth.
  • Drives Earth’s weather, seasons, and climate systems.
  • Causes solar winds and auroras due to solar activity.
